Campus life

Delhi Public School Kalinga Campus

Established in 2003, as a mission of the VidyaJyoti Trust in association with Delhi Public School Society.
The School provides education from Nursery to Class XII (Senior Secondary) and is affiliated to the Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE).
Set in an exuberant and lush green environment of 25 acres, this state- of –the- art school is located midway between the twin cities of Bhubaneswar and Cuttack. A fleet of 85 leveried buses network to cater to the transportation needs of the students and staff.
At DPS Kalinga we offer students contemporary and progressive schooling with utmost priority to high quality education. The school is equipped with state –of- the- art facilities and aims to develop students as ambassadors of positive change who will help to build a better tomorrow. With a balanced blend of curricular, co- curricular activities and community service the teaching learning process is enriched.

Vision And Mission
Vision And Mission

The aim of any good school should be to provide the best possible education to its students. This therefore, is the aim of Delhi Public School. Our primary aim is to develop in the student qualities of integrity, honesty trust tolerance, and compassion; to promote a spirit of enquiry to foster a scientific temper within the bonds of humanism; to help the student to become a meaningful part of his environment and to see that courage and industry have their due reward. The pursuit of excellence encouraged at DPS rests on the positive belief that every man has it in him to produce work, the quality of which can be described as first class.
Towards this end the school endeavours to provide a large and dedicated teaching staff with a wide range of skills and interests, a varied and wide choice of activities – academic, aesthetic, and athletic – an environment enriched with the excitement of discovery, challenge and competitiveness and discipline based on the belief that responsibility comes before freedom..

What's New

  • Carbon Emissions are Down Due to COVID19

    A group of 23 students went to the USA in May' 17 on an educational trip to NASA. The students took a four hour extensive training there, which included "Zero gravity experience, spinning in space, controlling an aircraft" etc. This was part of the 'Astronaut Training Experience'. A tour of the launch pad, rocket assembly unit was organised and the students got to see and touch the sample rock from the moon. They saw the original Atlantis Space Shuttle. This along with the fun trip around the Eastern Coast of America completed the tour. The students were escorted by Ms. Anulipi Mohapatra & Mr. Mustaque Ahmed.

  • DPS student commits suicide after failing exam

    Priyamdu, alias Gugu, hung herself in her house in Dwarka, southwest Delhi, on Tuesday after results of the Class 12 Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) exam were announced.
    According to police, the 17-year-old was rushed to the Ayushmaan Bhuva hospital by her family where she was declared dead.
    "Initially, the family did not want to inform the police fearing that the incident would bring a bad name to the family. The hospital authorities informed us about the death," said SK Tomar, assistant commissioner of police.
    This is the sixth incident in the last three months of a Class 12 student in Delhi committing suicide.
